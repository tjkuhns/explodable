# ADR-0008: Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) as a focus area — rejected

## Context

Evaluated GEO (Generative Engine Optimization — optimizing content for AI-search visibility, audit products for "how does your content appear in ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity search") as a potential focus area. At the time, GEO was emerging as a commercial category adjacent to the content-generation work already underway.

## Options considered

- **Pursue GEO as a product offering** — build GEO audits, visibility tracking, retainer services alongside existing content work.
- **Hybrid** — add GEO as a secondary service while maintaining the primary focus.
- **Reject** — stay focused on the existing anxiety-indexed KB + content-generation moat.

## Decision

Reject. GEO is misaligned with the engine's actual differentiator (anxiety-indexed cross-domain knowledge retrieval), and the market is commoditizing fast: Semrush, Ahrefs, and HubSpot are bolting GEO modules onto existing suites at $99–$200/month; dedicated tools (Otterly, Profound, Scrunch) are being priced into the monitoring tier. Early-mover window closes by late 2027.

## Consequences

GEO not pursued. No GEO products built; no GEO retainers offered; no GEO language in positioning. Any future re-proposal of GEO as a focus area requires a new ADR that addresses what changed about either the engine's moat or the GEO market since 2026-04-13. Do not re-raise in synthesis or planning responses.
