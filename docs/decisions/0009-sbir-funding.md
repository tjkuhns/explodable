# ADR-0009: SBIR Phase I funding path — rejected

## Context

Evaluated SBIR Phase I (NSF, $275K non-dilutive for AI topics) as a potential funding path. The engine's architecture (cognitive-task-aware multi-architecture pipeline, calibrated LLM-as-judge, novel KB schema) is a credible fit for NSF's AI topic category.

## Options considered

- **Apply** — 2–4 months of proposal writing + 6–12 month decision window + further 6–12 months to award. Uncertain outcome.
- **Don't apply** — direct the same operator-hours to portfolio + content + employment.
- **Apply in a future cycle** — defer until the project is in a state where the proposal writes itself.

## Decision

Reject (do not apply in the current cycle). Application backlog makes the effort-to-outcome ratio unfavorable. The operator-hours required to draft a competitive NSF proposal are not worth the opportunity cost against direct portfolio work + employment outreach.

## Consequences

SBIR not pursued. Funding strategy defaults to low-effort non-dilutive credits (NVIDIA Inception, OpenAI partner credits, etc.) and the primary goal shifts to employment income (AI engineering role). Any future re-proposal requires a new ADR that addresses what changed about the backlog timing or the operator's bandwidth. Do not re-raise SBIR in funding discussions.
